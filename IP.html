What is IP?

Intellectual property is any intangible asset that falls into one of the main four characters of patents, trademarks, copyrights, or trade secrets.

I. Patent

A patent is the right to exclude others from, producing, using, performing, or importing a new invention, new process, new article of manufacture, or new method within a governmental jurisdiction for a limited amount of time.

Example of Patent

II. Copyright

A copyright is an exclusive legal right that attaches as soon as a creation of original work is placed in a medium such as on paper, on a webpage, saved in computer memory etc. Some example of work that can be protected by copyright include but are not limited to literary works, musical compositions, computer programs, cell phone apps, and catch phrases and slogans. They can be denoted by this symbol Â©.

III. Trademark

A trademark is an easily recognizable sign, design, or type of expression that identifies certain products or services originating from a specific vendor.

Example of Trademark

IV. Trade Secret

A trade secret is a formula, practice, pattern, design, or any other composition of information which is not known or ascertainable by others that can give ne a certain competitive advantage in the marketplace. They are kept as a secret and are not released to the public to retain that competitive advantage permanently.
